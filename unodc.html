What is the UNODC?

The treatment of humans as chattel, the carefree use of drug corridors, the callous exploitation of people and the smuggling of drugs through fraudulent medicines - all these flagrant crimes against humanity rage unchallenged around the world.

The UNODC is a humanitarian appointed committee that aims to tackle two of the most vital, inhumane, and repulsive global issues.

The Committee Agenda

Addressing the heinous issue of Human and Drug Trafficking in Asia and Latin America.

Summary

The number of persons convicted of undertaking human trafficking per country has increased, with more than 30% of the victims being children. This number has been on a steady increase since the last decade, and is predicted to continue growing - internal strife, rising immigration, racism, and the suppression of women only making it easier.

Drug trafficking, on the other hand, is in itself a cause of innumerable threats, to the society as well as the environment. Drug trafficking has also proved to be resistant to previous measures taken, thus creating the need of an efficient and international plan of action.

With this agenda, the aim of the committee is to bring to light these issues and bring out potential solutions for the same. The wide scope of this agenda and its varied effects around the globe will provide every country with an opportunity to propose valuable solutions, spark healthy debates, and make this committee a harbinger of peace and justice.
