The Committee Agenda

Resolving the world refugee crisis with special emphasis on the MENA nations.

Summary

The Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees was established on December 14, 1950 by the United Nations General Assembly. The agency is mandated to lead and co-ordinate international action to protect refugees and resolve refugee problems worldwide. Its primary purpose is to safeguard the rights and well-being of refugees.

The Middle Eastern and African countries have faced the scourge of war from the likes of ISIS and Boko Haram. Thousands of people have lost their lives, their families and their livelihoods as a result. However, an important consequence of such wars, and an often neglected one, is the formation of refugees and Internally Displaced Persons who are forced to leave their homes. They travel to faraway lands with hardly anything with them where they live in refugee camps that have extremely poor sanitation. So many refugees live in fear of not getting accepted by other countries and getting deported back to their homelands and facing action there.

Something must be done. But by whom? Will the debaters be able to come up with a solution to all of the problems the refugees face? Will the countries play the blame game or will they come together? Join UNHCR and decide for yourself.
