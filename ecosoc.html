What is ECOSOC?

The Economic and Social Council (ECOSOC) is one of the six principal organs of the United Nations and is responsible for the direction and coordination of the economic, social, humanitarian activities carried out by the UN. It is the UN's largest and most complex subsidiary body.

The Committee Agenda

Discussing the impact of Neo-colonialism on Asian and African countries and finding solutions for combating the same.

Summary

Time and again, mankind finds itself on the crossroads of history wondering whether to learn from the past or to let it repeat itself.

The system of colonialism where settler nations ruled other nations had supposedly been eliminated in the twentieth century. At present, the world does not permit any country to aggressively breach the sovereignty of any other country.

Yet - decades after the commencement of decolonisation - the dominance of the west still haunts the Asian and African countries. This form of Imperialism is forged, not by the use of explicit political or military force but rather by implicit social, economic, cultural and linguistic measures of control.

The 21st century brings along with it a new and more lethal form of colonialism called neocolonialism. It refers to the practice of using capitalism and globalization; by promoting one’s own culture, language or media in the neo-colonies, after which corporations embedded in that culture can make greater headway in opening markets for themselves in those countries.

Neocolonialism would be the result of infiltrative business interests leading to deleterious cultural effects, thereby making the country lose its own identity. The questions that we shall concern ourselves with, relate to whether neocolonialism is a necessary evil or a formidable threat to national sovereignty and freedom. Is it possible to clearly demarcate which aspects of neo-colonialism promote global unity and which undermine it? If so, what are the solutions that present themselves to us?

The delegates in the commitee should think out of the box, have a liberal mindset and expect fiery debates. Welcome to ECOSOC!
