What is the UNEP?

The United Nations Environment Programme (UN Environment) is the leading global environmental authority that sets the global environmental agenda, promotes the coherent implementation of the environmental dimension of sustainable development within the United Nations system, and serves as an authoritative advocate for the global environment.

The Committee Agenda

Discussing the actions needed on a global scale to mitigate the impact of climate change.

Summary

Change is the only constant but climate change is the only undesirable change that the world wishes for.“Unless we take action on climate change, future generations will be roasted, toasted, fried and grilled.” It’s not just an unusually hot summer, it is climate change.

Our environment is constantly changing. There is no denying that. However, as our environment changes, so does the need to become increasingly aware of the problems that surround it. With a massive influx of natural disasters, warming and cooling periods, different types of weather patterns and much more, people need to be aware of what types of environmental problems our planet is facing.

We at UNEP expect the delegates to understand the gravity of the situation and what harm climate change can cause to mankind. Climate change is a global concern and needs immediate remedies to save us and our future generations from its impact. If you feel you have the courage to save the environment, come take part in the UNEP to present your ideas and innovative ways to tackle this global monster, climate change. Climate change is a result of human negligence and before we face its repercussions, we must try to lessen its impact.

Delegates can expect quality debate, backed by a fun and thrilling MUN experience, providing opportunities to think out of the box that will stir emotion and the zeal to protect, nourish and develop our mother earth.
